About Our Lab

Sudharma Learn Lab bridges the gap between academia and industry, introducing the transformative principles of Lean Thinking and Value Stream Mapping to the next generation of engineers. Established in collaboration between SGBIT, ITCube, and Avirays on 2nd February 2023, our mission extends beyond education. We're shaping industry-ready professionals through a blend of theory, real-world practice, and essential industry interactions. Powered by eVSM, a renowned tool for operational excellence, our vision is broad: to take this initiative across India, enhancing engineering education and bolstering the "Make In India" drive.

Team

Vision

Create a state-of-the-art lab powered by eVSM by creating a hub for collaboration and partnership, bringing together students, industry, and organizations to advance the practice of Lean Thinking

Mission

To provide a hands-on learning environment for students to develop practical skills in Lean Thinking and apply these principles to real-world problems. To serve as a resource for improving efficiency and effectiveness in engineering and industry through the application of Lean Thinking principles. To foster collaboration and partnerships with industry and organizations to provide students with practical experience and promote the adoption of Lean Thinking principles.

Functions

1. Set up a Lean Thinking lab or lab space: This could be a dedicated area where students can work on Lean Thinking projects and experiments. The lab could be equipped with tools and resources to support this

2.Offer a course on Lean Thinking: This can be a standalone course or part of a program that teaches students about the principles and practices of Lean Thinking.

3. Set up a Lean Thinking workshop series: These workshops can be focused on specific Lean Thinking tools and techniques, such as value stream mapping, 5S, and Kanban.

4. Create a Lean Thinking club: This club can bring together students who are interested in learning more about Lean Thinking and how it can be applied in engineering projects.

5.Develop a Lean Thinking capstone project: This could be a project that students work on as part of their coursework or as a part of a club or consulting group. The project could involve applying Lean Thinking principles to solve a real-world problem.

6.Invite guest speakers: Bring in experts in Lean Thinking to speak to students about their experiences using these principles in industry. This can provide valuable insights and real-world examples for students to learn from.

7. Establish a Lean Thinking consulting group: This group could work with engineering departments or companies to identify areas for improvement and implement Lean Thinking practices.
