⩤ Course Structure ⩥

1.Beginners Level: Pre Final Year Students

1. Lean and VSM Basics:

    I. Topics Covered:


  • ● Understanding Lean Principles
  • ● Identifying Different Forms of Waste
  • ● Introduction to Value Stream Mapping (VSM)
  • ● The Need and Benefits of VSM
  • ● Overview of Other Lean Tools, including:
    • ⇒ 5S: Workplace organization method
    • ⇒ Poka-Yoke: Mistake-proofing techniques
    • ⇒ Kaizen: Continuous improvement process
    • ⇒ Kanban: Visual workflow management method
    • ⇒ SMED: Single-Minute Exchange of Die, quick changeover techniques
  • ● Introduction to VSM for:
    • ⇒ Production
    • ⇒ Services
    • ⇒ Supply Chain

2. Fast Draw and Time Map:

I.Topics Covered:


  • ● Mastery in Fast Draw Techniques
  • ● Proficiency in Time Mapping
  • ● Real-time Application: Mapping the Process Flow in Various Businesses

3. Spaghetti Analysis:

I.Topics Covered:


  • ● Basics of Spaghetti Analysis
  • ● Application in Measuring Distance Traveled by Operator or Material in a Plant
  • ● Understanding the Importance of Flow Efficiency

4. PDCA Improvement Framework:

I.Topics Covered:


  • ● Introduction to the Plan-Do-Check-Act (PDCA) Cycle
  • ● How PDCA Framework Integrates with VSM for Continuous Improvement

5. Project Planning:

I.Topics Covered:


  • ● Introduction to Basic Project Planning Tools
  • ● Handling Project Timelines
  • ● Effective Resource Staffing Strategies for VSM Projects

The "Beginners Level" offers a comprehensive foundation that will ensure students are well-equipped to tackle the more advanced topics and real-world scenarios presented in the subsequent levels.

II. Advanced Level: Designed for Final Year Students

    1. Plant Production Maps:

    ● Topics Covered:


  • ⇒ Deep Dive into Value Stream Mapping for Mixed Model Plant (Manufacturing and Process-oriented Industry)
  • ⇒ Advanced Techniques for VSM in Manufacturing
  • ⇒ Application in Diverse Manufacturing Scenarios

2. Transaction Maps (Service Maps):

    ● Topics Covered:


    ● Advanced Mapping for Service Industries, including:

  • ⇒ Software Development
  • ⇒ Services and Staffing
  • ⇒ Customer Journey Mapping

3. Supply Network Mapping:

    ● Topics Covered:


  • ⇒ Detailed Value Stream Mapping of Entire Supply Chains
  • ⇒ Techniques for Mapping from Various Suppliers to Multiple Customers
  • ⇒ Advanced Tools and Techniques for Supply Chain Mapping

4. Real-Time Industrial Case Studies:

    ● Process and Learning:


  • ⇒ Direct Industry Engagement: Students collaborate with industries to build case studies.
  • ⇒ Process Understanding: Students visit industries to comprehend and analyze their processes.
  • ⇒ Data Collection: Walk-throughs of industry processes for hands-on data collection.
  • ⇒ Mapping using eVSM: Transforming collected data into actionable maps using eVSM software.
  • ⇒ Collaborative Brainstorming: Engage with industry teams to discuss and analyze current state findings.
  • ⇒ Future State Design: Based on findings and brainstorming, students design the future state of the industry process.
  • ⇒ Report and Action Plan: Prepare a comprehensive comparison report between current and future states, concluding with a robust implementation action plan.

This structure for the "Advanced Level" emphasizes not just the theoretical and technical aspects but also underscores the real-world application and practical experiences that students will gain. The direct engagement with the industry and the hands-on approach will equip students with invaluable insights and skills as they transition into professionals.
